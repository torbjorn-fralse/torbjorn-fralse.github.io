Observational Report  ·  2026 (Draft)

Megaliths of a Tilted World

How multiple independent datasets reveal a great circle pattern across ancient sites.

A tilted great circle fits the distribution of ancient monumental stone construction better than the equator or any parallel. As construction quality rises, the fit sharpens. The pattern survives leave-one-out tests, Monte Carlo simulations against random points on land, and shuffles of the quality variables. The geometry is real. The cause is not addressed.

About

Torbjörn Frälse writes speculative fiction and observational reports. The work sits in the space where the documented record thins out, where coincidences ask to be counted, and where the answer is sometimes that the geometry is real and the cause is not yet addressed.

The data behind every empirical claim is public. The code that produced every chart is public. Disagreement is welcome.
